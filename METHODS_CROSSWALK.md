## 2. Approach taken, per problem

- **Estimate a rare prevalence without reviewing 90k docs** → stratify with a cheap noisy screen, census the small flagged stratum, sample the unflagged mass, combine with Horvitz-Thompson; bound the unobserved with Beta-Binomial / Clopper-Pearson. (Design-based, not model-based — fewer assumptions.)
- **The lexical screen can't see its own false-negatives** → add a *non-lexical* judge whose errors are independent of keyword matching (embedding/LLM), so the second instrument estimates the first's misses.
- **Trust the judge?** → don't assume; benchmark. When no gold is affordable, use a *cross-family* panel and recover accuracy + prevalence with a latent-class model (Hui-Walter), then check the conditional-independence assumption explicitly.
- **A weak local model hedges** → few-shot anchoring with consensus exemplars (incl. keyword-matched-negatives) — calibration before capability.
- **Rediscover the construct's surface, not hand-pick keywords** → mine the high-agreement positive/negative sets with Fightin' Words + log-likelihood collocations; treat the output as hypotheses to validate, not truth.
- **Avoid per-item human labeling at scale** → latent-class no-gold estimation; push human effort to (i) the construct definition/rules and (ii) a tiny reusable audit (existing coded cases), not O(n) labels.
